The Roster

Meet your agents

Every agent has one job, one place in the hierarchy, and the cheapest model that does that job well. High-stakes work goes to Claude; routine work stays local.

Claude · high-stakes

team-leader

Owns your goals. Breaks them into tasks, delegates to the right agent, reviews what comes back, and compiles the nightly Telegram report.

"plan tonight's backlog and delegate"
local qwen · routine

dev-worker

Writes code, fixes failing tests, and bumps dependencies. Works in small, reviewable diffs so nothing lands unchecked.

"fix failing test in attendance.service"
Claude · high-stakes

code-reviewer

Reviews every diff before it reaches you. Flags correctness bugs, risky patterns, and anything that needs a human decision.

"review PR #47 for correctness"
local qwen · routine

test-writer

Backfills coverage for untested paths and writes regression tests for every bug the team fixes, so nothing breaks twice.

"add tests for the payments module"
local qwen · routine Planned

report-writer

Turns the audit log into human-readable summaries: weekly progress reports, changelogs, and stakeholder updates.

"draft this week's changelog"
local qwen · routine Planned

monitor

Watches your services and pipelines. Notices failures before your users do and files an issue with the relevant logs attached.

"alert me if the API error rate spikes"

One chain of command

Agents don't freelance. Every one of them sits in a six-level hierarchy — orchestrator at the top, tool executor at the bottom — so every task has an owner and every result has an audit trail.
